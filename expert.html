Expert App

Project for Careerfoundry UX design program. An app that enables anyone, anywhere to instantly chat with an expert in virtually any field. Check out the app's project brief.

The problem

Using the Double Diamond Strategy it was possible to identify a series of possible problems/hypotheses that the idea of this app would aim to solve. The below problem statement was later refined and identified:

Problem Statement

HR students need a simple and easy way to network, ask questions and get mentorship to break the gap between student-life to work-life. They need an environment that is welcoming, private (no sensitive data is shared online), and where quick and reliable feedback is key. They also want to ensure cultural bias and geographic differences don’t influence the credibility of the interactions provided by professionals.

We will know this to be true when we see increased user adoption by HR students of this app; users ranking professionals’ answers and professionals actively registering and engaging with students by sharing their experiences and knowledge.

The Solution

Expert app allows users to browse and research HR professionals based on their location, level of experience and knowledge of HR policies.

Through the app, users can easily connect with professionals, asking questions and requesting video/audio conversations, empowering their network and preparing them for their chosen careers.

5. Visual Design

Visual design principles & Accessibility design:

The app design was revisited following the 3 Principles of Design and Gestalt Properties/Laws of grouping. Most screens were considered under these principles in order to achieve symmetry and ease of use.

Similarly, screens were checked for accessibility, lifting up and correcting any immediate issues that could be posed for specific audiences and minimizing poor app usage/adoption by specific audiences.

Peer collaboration:

Taking advantage of Careerfoundry network, the app final prototype was presented to designers and design students, who provided valuable insight and identified areas of improvement to the app. These were implemented and corrected.

Language Design Systems:

When preparing deliverables for production, a Language Design System, identifying Expert app visual language has been created, highlighting usage of colour, components (images and text) for the app.

Learnings

Throughout this exercise, I was able to learn all stages of a UX design project. Although some areas were familiar to me, such as research and persona development, these were done slightly differently from my past experience and a lot of emphases was placed on how a user interacts with the design.

I've also learned how to use systems that I haven't used before, such as Adobe XD; Figma; Whimsical and other tools that assisted me throughout my research. Looking out for good tutorials was the most difficult part of the project as I had to familiarize myself with UX terminology and understand who to look out for in terms of inspiration and advice.

Overall it was a great experience, and one I'm now able to cross with my marketing career, building up on new products and campaigns.
